The customer flow

Customer chooses a product

They click the product card and pay through that product's PayPal link.

Customer submits delivery details

The form link sends address details into your Google Sheet order dashboard.

You fulfill with supplier

You use the supplier link in the admin list to place the order using the customer's address.

Profit formula

This version uses a markup approach. If supplier landed cost is C, target selling price is roughly C × 1.5 or higher. If you want a true 50% gross margin after cost, the formula is C ÷ 0.5, which is 2C before payment fees.

Example

Supplier cost $10. A 50% markup gives $15 selling price. A true 50% margin requires $20 selling price. Payment fees, refunds, and ad spend reduce real profit.

Automation reality.

This static website cannot secretly auto-buy AliExpress products by itself. The free practical path is PayPal notification + Google Sheet + DSers/AliExpress fulfillment.

Free static automation

Payment notification, form capture, Google Sheet dashboard, daily fulfillment. Lowest cost. Some manual work remains.

DSers route

Use DSers to map AliExpress products, bulk order, and sync tracking after supplier shipment. Best once orders appear.

Full auto route

Requires Shopify/WooCommerce/Wix integration or custom API/webhook work. Not truly zero-cost and should wait until sales are proven.
